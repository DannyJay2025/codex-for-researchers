# Manuscript Polishing Style Guide

## General Style

- Prefer precise verbs over inflated phrasing.
- Keep topic sentences clear and specific.
- Use cautious language for mechanisms, causality, and clinical impact unless the evidence is explicit.
- Avoid marketing-style adjectives such as groundbreaking, revolutionary, powerful, or unparalleled.
- Avoid filler openings such as "It is well known that" and "In recent years" unless they add context.

## Claim Discipline

Use:

- "suggests" when evidence is indirect.
- "is associated with" for observational relationships.
- "improves" only when the source reports a direct improvement.
- "may" when the conclusion depends on future validation.

Avoid:

- "proves" for most biological, clinical, or computational studies.
- "first" unless novelty has been verified.
- "significant" unless it refers to statistical significance or clearly defined practical importance.

## Revision Notes

When useful, explain edits in three groups:

- Logic: argument order, missing bridge, unclear contrast.
- Language: concision, grammar, terminology.
- Risk: unsupported claim, overstatement, missing citation, ambiguous statistic.
